How much new capacity would be installed?

The model estimates how much new capacity is installed, given the chosen amount of investment and the energy mix. This estimate is calculated using IPCC’s figures “overnight costs” (in USD/kW), which indicates the capital cost, excluding finance cost for construction. In short, it indicates the cost of building a power plant overnight as if no interest were incurred. Please refer to the report for a detailed explanations of the methodology, and the limits of the estimate. Source of data: http://www.ipcc.ch/pdf/assessment-report/ar5/wg3/ipcc_wg3_ar5_annex-iii.pdf

The result is compared with the average capacity of existing and under construction nuclear reactors in the world, which is 922 MW (1th june 2015). Source: European Nuclear Association, http://www.euronuclear.org/info/encyclopedia/n/nuclear-power-plant-world-wide.htm

The result is also compared with the average nameplate capacity of a coal unit in the US, which is 272 MW (2013). Source: US’s Energy Information Administration, http://www.eia.gov/electricity/annual/ (Table 4.3)
